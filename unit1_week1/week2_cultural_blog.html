Exploring your values

When I think of the times in my life where I have been the happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied, these are the following things that come to my mind:

  • Accomplishment
  • Personal development
  • Competition
  • Status
  • Music
  • Money

The value that is most important to me is Accomplishment. I feel very satisfied when I accomplish my goals - be it at work, school or playing a game. Accomplishment isn't about winning the ultimate prize. It is the satisfaction of completing a task successfully by putting in my best efforts. There have been times in my life when I know that I could have put in a little more effort and achieved my goals but I try to improve myself with every goal I set for myself.

In general I try to live up to these values:

  • Accomplishment: 3
  • Personal development: 4
  • Competition: 3
  • Status: 3
  • Music: 3
  • Money: 4

When he fist joined the school it was a big change for him having to move from Boston to Georgetown. He had no friends in Georgetown and his first month in MBA school was though. I advised him to not panic and take it easy and focus on studying.
